The 95% Discount

On 9 December 2022, H.G. Perseus GmbH StBG acquired 158,208 shares of SyncPilot SE at €18.96 per share, paying €3,000,004. The board set the implied company valuation at €100 million.

Three months later, on 14 March 2023, the same entity acquired 127,885 additional shares at €1.00 per share — paying just €127,885. The implied valuation collapsed to €5.4 million.

Two months after that, on 11 May 2023, Tiven GmbH acquired shares at €20.56 per share — implying a valuation of €112 million. On 5 June 2023, Perseus itself bought 16,211 more shares at €20.56.

Same buyer, same company: 95% price collapse in 95 days
Source: Handelsregister capital-event filings
What happened between December 2022 and March 2023 that justified a 95% price collapse? And what happened between March and May that justified a 2,000% rebound?
Nothing visible. No restructuring. No new anchor clients. No bankruptcy filing. No market shock. Revenue didn't change. The product didn't change. The clients didn't change. The only thing that changed was who was buying — and what price the board chose to set.

The Question

H.G. Perseus GmbH StBG acquired shares at three different prices over six months. Two of those prices matched what every other investor paid. One did not.

Who owns H.G. Perseus? What is their history? And why did the board grant them — and only them — a 95% discount?

The answers involve two Munich lawyers, a tax fraud prosecution, and a second deal that went wrong.
