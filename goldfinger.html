The Goldfinger Case

The "Goldfinger" scheme exploited precious metals trades to generate fictitious tax losses, artificially reducing taxable income. German prosecutors described it as one of the country's largest tax fraud cases. Both Hunstein and Goerg were charged as defendants. Both saw their charges dropped in 2020. No conviction resulted.

Source: German press reporting. Charges dropped per court records, 2020. No source document in this dossier independently verifies the "largest" characterisation.
