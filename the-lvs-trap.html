The Deal

On 21 May 2021, LVS Hotel & Resorts S.A. entered a Cooperation and Call Option Agreement with SyncPilot's founder, Franz-Xaver Bleicher. The terms: €18,750,000 to be paid in tranches, in exchange for call options over 10,923 shares (43.69%) in Quintus Beteiligungs GmbH — a holding structure that in turn held 15% of SyncPilot.

The Augsburg Regional Court has since characterised the agreement as a composite contract: several independent but economically interrelated contracts forming a single unit, and embedding within it a loan from LVS to Bleicher in the principal amount of €18.75 million, with a term to 31 December 2024. The court expressly rejected Bleicher's argument that the loan was merely "a purely technical financing vehicle."

Martin Hunstein of GHR Goerg Hunstein Rechtsanwälte drafted the agreement. He served as legal advisor to the transaction. Hunstein — together with his partner Diethard Goerg — owns H.G. Perseus GmbH StBG, the entity that would acquire SyncPilot shares at a 95% discount twenty-two months later.

In August 2021, LVS requested a modification of the Cooperation and Call Option Agreement, reducing the loan amount to €7,500,000 in exchange for 375,000 shares in the holding structure (Option 1 in the original Cooperation and Call Option Agreement). Hunstein drafted the modification agreement.

The €6.15 Million Question: Where Did the Money Go?

LVS transferred €6,150,000 to SyncPilot's founder between April and August 2021. In return, LVS received zero shares, zero equity, and — until the Augsburg Regional Court intervened in May 2026 — zero repayment.

Franz-Xaver Bleicher — SyncPilot's sole Managing Director and largest beneficial owner — controlled the allocation of all incoming funds. The following facts are documented:

1. The money entered SyncPilot's orbit. All four tranches are confirmed by bank transfer records and by the Augsburg Regional Court. Total: €6,150,000.

2. No shares were issued. The Handelsregister shows no capital event corresponding to the LVS payments. Zero shares were allocated to LVS or any entity connected to LVS.

3. Bleicher acquired personal assets during this period. Evidence indicates Bleicher purchased residential property and bought out a co-owner's stake in the BOSYNC holding structure during the same months that LVS funds were flowing in. The source of capital for these personal acquisitions — and whether it included loan funds designated for corporate purposes — is the central question a forensic audit must answer.

Under German law, a managing director who diverts corporate funds to personal use commits Untreue (breach of fiduciary duty, §266 StGB) — punishable by up to five years' imprisonment. If the funds were solicited under false representations, this additionally constitutes Betrug (fraud, §263 StGB).
