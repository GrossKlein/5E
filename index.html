SyncPilot

An investigative dossier into the company that handles digital consultations for German federal agencies — and the people who control it.

€6.15M lent by LVS Hotel & Resorts S.A. — zero shares delivered. On 13 May 2026 the Augsburg Regional Court ordered repayment — €6,706,610.38 with interest — and the creditor froze his assets across eight targets the same day. The lawyer who drafted the contract acquired shares in the same firm at a 95% discount. That same lawyer was a Goldfinger tax fraud defendant. Among the assets now frozen is a €1.75M land charge at 15% interest over a property at the managing director's registered address of record. 84% of revenue comes from German taxpayers. In late 2024, Pictet Asset Management extended debt financing to the company — eighteen months after shares had traded at €1 and the CEO was pitching a €250M valuation on €12M revenue.
