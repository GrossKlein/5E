SyncPilot SE

An investigative dossier into the company that handles digital consultations for German federal agencies — and the people who control it.

€6.15 million paid by an investor. Zero shares delivered. The contract was never notarized — by the lawyer who drafted it.

That same lawyer's company acquired shares in SyncPilot at a 95% discount — paying €1.00 per share when every other investor paid €18–21.

That same lawyer was a defendant in the Goldfinger tax fraud prosecution, one of the largest in German history.

The company's sole managing director — who controlled all incoming funds — acquired personal property during the period investor money was flowing in.

This company's clients include the Bundesagentur für Arbeit, AOK (Allgemeine Ortskrankenkasse), and the Senatskanzlei Berlin. 84% of its revenue comes from German taxpayers.

Public Sector Exposure

SyncPilot SE's software is embedded in digital consultation systems across German federal, state, and municipal agencies. 84.3% of its €44.2M projected revenue comes from three taxpayer-funded sectors: public administration, social insurance, and health insurance.

Top clients include: Bundesagentur für Arbeit · AOK (Allgemeine Ortskrankenkasse) (11 regional offices) · Senatskanzlei Berlin · ekom21 · ITSG · AKDB Bayern · Deutsche Rentenversicherung · SVLFG · Volkswagen · Vodafone · E.ON · AXA

SyncPilot holds a Silver partnership with SAP (downgraded from Gold), through which it reaches enterprise procurement cycles.
