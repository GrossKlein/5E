SyncPilot

An investigative dossier into the company that handles digital consultations for German federal agencies — and the people who control it.

€6.15M paid by an investor — zero shares delivered. The lawyer behind the contract acquired shares at a 95% discount. That same lawyer was a Goldfinger tax fraud defendant. The sole managing director acquired personal property while investor money flowed in. 84% of revenue comes from German taxpayers. In late 2024, Pictet Asset Management extended debt financing to the company — eighteen months after shares had traded at €1 and the CEO was pitching a €250M valuation on €12M revenue.
