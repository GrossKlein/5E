The Company

Summary
SyncPilot SE sells consultation software to German government agencies. 84% of its €44 million revenue comes from taxpayer-funded bodies — Bundesagentur für Arbeit, AOK (Allgemeine Ortskrankenkasse), Senatskanzlei Berlin. It carries a paper valuation of €128 million. It routes 100% of its equity through 26 holding entities across six jurisdictions. One man controls it all: founder and Managing Director Franz-Xaver Bleicher.

The Business

SyncPilot develops software for guided online sales consultations. Its core product enables co-browsing, video conferencing, and screen-sharing between sales agents and customers. The software targets regulated industries — insurance, public administration, healthcare — where compliance demands that the customer sees exactly what the agent sees, in real time.

The client base is overwhelmingly German public-sector and quasi-public. The five largest clients — Bundesagentur für Arbeit, ekom21, Senatskanzlei Berlin, ITSG, and AKDB Bayern — account for 41% of revenue. Health insurance bodies (led by the AOK (Allgemeine Ortskrankenkasse) network across eleven regional offices) and social insurance organisations contribute another 42%. Enterprise names appear on the roster — Volkswagen, Vodafone, AXA, E.ON — but together they account for less than 15% of the €44.2M projected client revenue for 2024/25.

SyncPilot holds a Silver partnership with SAP, downgraded from Gold. The partnership lends procurement credibility — but it also means that German public agencies are integrating SyncPilot into SAP-ecosystem contracts worth six figures or more.

Who Controls This Company

Franz-Xaver Bleicher commands 16.48% — the single largest effective stake — entirely through his holding company BOSYNC Beteiligungs UG, where he serves as Geschäftsführer (managing director) and holds 70% directly. The next two largest beneficiaries, Dr. Ingo Krocke (8.65%) and Harald Steiner (8.58%), route their interests through Attegia Holding Ltd. in Malta and AURIGA Capital AG in Switzerland, respectively. Together, these three individuals control over a third of SyncPilot SE without their names appearing directly on the company's share register.

In total, 31 ultimate beneficial owners hold shares through 26 entities across six jurisdictions: 🇩🇪 🇲🇹 🇱🇺 🇨🇭 🇱🇮 — Germany (18), Malta (4), Luxembourg (2), Switzerland (1), and Liechtenstein (1). No share has ever changed hands through a public exchange or independent third-party valuation.

Bleicher founded SyncPilot and has served as its sole Managing Director since formation. He held 50.9% of the company at incorporation in September 2020. By August 2025, his effective stake had dropped to 23.5% — yet he remains the single largest beneficial owner and the only person with operational control of the company.

During the period from April to August 2021 — while €6.15 million in investor funds flowed into SyncPilot — Bleicher purchased residential property and bought out a co-owner's stake in the holding structure. The source of funds for these personal acquisitions during the same period investor capital was entering the company is a central question of this dossier. Hard documentation is under investigation; the timeline overlap is established.

Every share price in SyncPilot's history was set by the board Bleicher controls. No independent valuation has ever been commissioned. No share has ever traded on a public exchange. The company's €128 million paper valuation exists only because Bleicher's board says it does.

Board-set share prices swung from €18.96 to €1.00 and back in 150 days

SyncPilot SE has conducted ten capital events between September 2020 and August 2025. The share price per event — set by the board, never by an independent valuation — ranged from €1.00 to €23.64. One event stands out: on 14 March 2023, H.G. Perseus acquired 127,885 shares at €1.00 each, implying a company valuation of €5.4M — a 95% discount from the €100M valuation just three months earlier.

Revenue Snapshot

SyncPilot's projected annual recurring revenue climbed from €6.9M in January 2024 to €25.5M by December 2025 — a 268% increase on paper. The client list spans 55 organisations, but revenue concentration is extreme: the top five clients account for 41%, and the public sector alone generates 42% of all revenue.
